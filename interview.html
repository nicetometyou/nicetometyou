Interview with the Head of educational services at the MET and visit report

Unfortunately the MET is closed since September 2017 for building maintenance reasons. I couldn't interview the museum's users, but I did a long interview with the head of educational services at the MET, Mrs. Federica Foschi.
I also had the opportunity to visit the room that housed the museum to better understand how the museum was organized; you will find the report of the visit. The renovation of the museum is expected to start soon.

Note:
MP= Michela Parma
FF= Federica Foschi

Question no. 1

MP: Who are the users who mostly visit the museum? What is the period of the year in which you have the most audience?

FF: The most frequent visitors were schools during the school year, from nursery schools to lower secondary schools; the classes came both to make the visit and to carry out laboratory activities. However, we also had tourists in the summer period, let's say however that the period concerning the school year was that of greater turnout.

Question no. 2

MP: What kind of educational activities does the museum offer?

FF: The educational activities we offer for schools are available at the following link http://www.museisantarcangelo.it/didattica/. The activities, before the museum closed for renewal in September 2017, took place in room C, "conference room and educational workshops". The activities that take place in class include two-hour wokshops related to the art, archeology, history and traditions of Romagna.

Question no. 3

MP: Did you offer guided tours? If yes, how were they structured?

FF: We offered guided tours: we had entrusted the reception service, ticket office, bookshop and guided tours to a cooperative of Rimini. The staff had been trained by the director of the municipal museums. The visit included the entire museum itinerary, divided into 10 sections, and structured according to users.

Question no. 4

MP: Do you offer collateral events? If yes, to whom are they addressed? How much is the participation? Which tool do you use for event communication?

FF: Events and projects related to the museum:

  • PAM CLUB Little friends of the museums of Santarcangelo (2019: 20 years of Pam club): every year we propose a project with meetings, workshops, shows, readings for children aged 3 to 10 years and their families (in each appointment there are 20/30 people between adults and children);
  • PUPPET FESTIVAL: in 2019 we arrived at the 29th edition; summer and winter show of puppet shows of street theater tradition (each show sees the participation of about 300 people);
  • NIGHT AT THE MUSEUM: in a tent in the park of the museum and spend a night together (2019: 5th year);
  • REMUSRETE project of the MUSEUMS OF TWO RIVERS (Marecchia and Uso): the remus network is a union of 4 municipalities - Santarcangelo, Poggio Torriana, Verucchio, Bellaria Igea Marina - which through a memorandum of understanding carries out projects of cultural sharing, enhancement cultural and territorial heritage.
  • Finally, in collaboration with local associations we offer aperitifs, music, parties.

Communication tools: Facebook pages, newsletters, press releases, instagram, mail, printed material: posters, leaflets, etc.

Question no. 5

MP: Did the museum itinerary involve the active participation of the user? Was it possible to handle or try to use traditional Romagna tools?

FF: No, the museum tour did not include the active participation of the users. It often happened that visitors, especially children, touch the instruments and tools, because they are attractive, but there was no tactile path.

Question no. 6

MP: Was there digital tools?

FF: There were, in some sections, monitors with videos linked to the section: eg videos on the blacksmith, videos on the mills of the Valmarecchia, etc.

Question no. 7

MP: What are the strengths of the museum that attract visitors?

FF: The strengths are the objects themselves, which relate to the history, traditions and culture of the southern Romagna region, so the knowledge and discovery of the use of these tools fascinates a lot, in particular the immaterial aspect, that is the whole rituality, gestures, symbolism linked to these objects and to everyday life, work, social and family life.

Question no. 8

MP: What aspects of the museum should be improved and how could they be improved?

FF: The multimedia aspect of the museum must certainly be enhanced and developed. Surely with the start of the redevelopment works, a multimedia design will be evaluated.

Question no. 9

MP: Do you have collaborations with local institutions or artisans?

FF: We have collaborations with the solidarity buying group of Santarcangelo (GAS), which have their headquarters in a local museum through an agreement.

Question no. 10

MP: Are there little-used or empty spaces where it would be possible to improve the visit to the museum with multimedia installations or immersive reality experiences?

FF: No, spaces are all used.

Question no. 11

MP: What kind of documents are kept in the museum's archives and library? Have they been digitised?

FF: All books that were kept in the museum library were transferred to the municipal library A. Baldini. We have numerous documents which have not been digitised but which can be consulted, upon request, by students and researchers. For the photographic funds we had started a digitisation project which unfortunately came to a halt about 3 years ago due to force majeure. Finally, the documents in the video library have been converted from VHS to DVD and those in the audio library have been converted from cassettes to CDs.
